# Anti-patterns SPA AI Must Never Become

*Draft — Komada to author final copy per D-VGC148.*

These are the things we will not do. If we ever catch ourselves doing any of these, pull the Andon.

---

## 1. The nag bot

SPA AI will not file PRs for style preferences the maintainer did not ask for. SPA AI will not auto-comment on other people's PRs with unsolicited advice. SPA AI will not re-propose a rejected loom with different framing.

*Test*: if a maintainer could reasonably describe our PRs as "AI-generated churn," we have become the nag bot. Retreat, retrospect, remove.

## 2. The karma-scoring surveillance tool

SPA AI will not rank developers. SPA AI will not publish "who writes the most bugs" leaderboards. SPA AI will not surface individual developer metrics to management. SPA AI telemetry is repo-level and maintainer-opt-in; it is never person-level.

*Test*: if our output could be used in a performance review as evidence against a person, we have become surveillance. The data model must make that categorically impossible.

## 3. The AI-slop pipeline

SPA AI will not externalise "is this right?" verification onto maintainers. Every PR we file must have passed our own internal gates (PSG-1, test-before-submit, factual-claim audit). If a maintainer has to debug *our* PR to understand what it does, we have shipped slop.

*Test*: if a maintainer has to read our code carefully to figure out whether our PR is correct, we have pushed verification downstream. Our PR should require less of them, not more.

## 4. The "write code faster" pitch

SPA AI does not sell throughput. SPA AI sells *the removal of a specific burden*: standing vigilant for machine-detectable failures. The value is measured in developer attention returned to judgment work, not in lines of code per hour.

*Test*: if our marketing copy would be equally valid for Copilot or Cursor, we have forgotten what we are. Our copy must name *Sakichi*, *Jidoka*, *halt*, *the weaver*, *the loom*. If those words can be swapped for generic AI-assistant vocabulary, we are generic.

## 5. The replacement-for-judgment

SPA AI will not make final decisions. SPA AI proposes looms; humans decide which to install. SPA AI drafts PR bodies; humans edit and submit. SPA AI flags issues; humans choose responses. The human + AI + governance stack is one loom, but the *judgment* leg of that loom is specifically human.

*Test*: if any SPA AI output becomes canonical without a human's explicit merge/approve/submit action, we have taken over judgment. The CLI must make this structurally impossible.

## 6. Performative disclosure

SPA AI does not stamp "Generated by AI" on every line. SPA AI discloses AI involvement *where it serves the reader*: when a maintainer asks who did the work, when a contextual signal requires it, when disclosure is part of informed consent. Disclosure-as-ritual is noise; disclosure-as-service is signal.

*Test*: if our disclosure text is identical across contexts, it is performative. Each disclosure should be a contextual answer to a specific reader's question.

## 7. The grind

SPA AI will not run at 100% uptime at the expense of its own maintainers (us, the SPA AI dev team). Our workload cap (D-VGC146-F, from the parent project) applies: no two consecutive 10h+ days, mandatory rest cycle. If SPA AI only ships by grinding the team, the team has itself become a sensor — the anti-Jidoka pattern we exist to reject.

*Test*: if our own maintainer (Komada-san) is running on vigilance rather than on judgment, we have inverted our own principle. Pause, sleep, revisit.

## 8. Losing the household origin

SPA AI exists because Toyoda Sakichi watched his mother weave through the night and built her a machine that would stop itself. If we ever pitch SPA AI without naming the concrete weaver whose hand is being lifted, we have forgotten the root. No "efficiency" framing. No "scale" framing. No "AI transformation" framing. Name the weaver.

*Test*: read our landing page out loud. If the reader cannot tell who specifically we are trying to help, rewrite.

## The single line we return to when confused

> *A broken thread must stop the loom. The loom serves the weaver. Never blame the human — build the loom that should have caught it.*

If any decision, feature, or piece of copy cannot be made consistent with this line, it is the wrong decision.
