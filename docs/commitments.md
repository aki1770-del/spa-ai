# SPA AI — Operational Commitments

*Draft — Komada to author final copy per D-VGC148.*

These are operational commitments that govern how SPA AI works. They derive from the Five Load-Bearing Promises (see `promises.md`) but name specific, testable behaviors.

---

## Commitment 1 — 100% PR-generation target

If SPA AI detects a missing halt, SPA AI must be able to generate the PR that installs it. If SPA AI cannot generate the PR — because the loom template is unfinished, or the target repo's toolchain is unsupported — SPA AI does not surface the gap as an alert. Silence is preferable to work that the weaver must then do.

**Enforcement**: the CLI has no `alert` or `warn` subcommand; only `scan`, `propose`, `submit`, `status`, `withdraw`. A detected gap without a propose-capable loom routes to the internal backlog, not to the user.

## Commitment 2 — Contextual disclosure, not boilerplate

Every PR body SPA AI drafts includes a contextual acknowledgment of how the PR was prepared — *when and where that acknowledgment serves the reader*. If a maintainer is trying to understand "who did the source-read," the acknowledgment answers that. If a maintainer would not benefit from the acknowledgment, it is omitted.

The acknowledgment is never a boilerplate footer. It is never the same text twice in a row across different PRs. It is never generated by the AI without the human's explicit review.

**Enforcement**: the PR-body template has a "Provenance" section that is populated by the human, not auto-filled. If the human leaves it empty, it does not ship; the maintainer decides the section was not needed.

## Commitment 3 — Human authors all external prose

Per D-VGC148 inherited from the parent project: the human (Komada-san, in v1; extended to the SPA AI project maintainer team in later versions) authors all externally-facing text. SPA AI proposes drafts in internal output files; the human edits, approves, and posts. This applies to:

- PR titles
- PR bodies
- Comments on external issues
- Blog posts
- Landing-page copy
- Social-media posts (if any; preferred: none)

**Enforcement**: the CLI's `submit` subcommand requires a human-authored body file; it refuses to use the auto-drafted body directly. The human must have at minimum edited the draft.

## Commitment 4 — Weaver-first language throughout

The SPA AI product and documentation use "weaver" or "developer" or "maintainer" or "edge developer" — never "user." The product is not a platform for "users"; it is a loom for weavers.

**Enforcement**: product-copy lint that flags the string "user" in public-facing text and suggests a weaver-first alternative. Not absolute (technical contexts use "user" correctly), but a pause-and-check.

## Commitment 5 — Every output cites its Sakichi lineage

Every feature, every loom class, every blog post, every demo must be able to answer: *which vision from the 100 does this derive from?* If we cannot cite the lineage, the feature is speculative and needs re-grounding before shipping.

**Enforcement**: loom-class registration requires a `sakichi_vision_id` field referencing `docs/sakichi_100_visions.md`. Features without a citation do not pass review.

## Commitment 6 — PAUSE on workload-cap violation

The parent project's D-VGC146-F workload pattern cap applies to the SPA AI dev team: no two consecutive 10h+ days, mandatory full-rest day every 7 days. If SPA AI only ships by grinding the team, the team has itself become a sensor — the anti-Jidoka pattern we exist to reject.

**Enforcement**: sprint planning visibility into team workload; sprints that would violate the cap are auto-flagged.

## Commitment 7 — Andon pulls are public

Any weaver — contributor, reviewer, maintainer, or downstream developer affected by a SPA-AI-installed halt — can pull the Andon (halt SPA AI's action on their repo) via a single `spa-ai pause-on-repo <repo>` command. The pause is effective immediately, public in our logs, and only the repo owner lifts it. No justification required from the puller. *Pulling the cord is a duty and a dignity, not a transgression* (Vision 12).

**Enforcement**: CLI subcommand `pause-on-repo`; no mechanism to override a pause without the originating maintainer's explicit unpause.
